Saudi Football Federation — AI Player Performance

Computer Vision · Full Pipeline · 2nd & 3rd Division + U18

Computer Vision YOLO Player Tracking Speed Detection Ball Possession Pass Detection Python Data Pipeline Labeling Database Low-Quality Video

Overview

  • Built a full AI and computer vision system for the Saudi Football Federation to automatically analyze player performance during matches.
  • Covers 2nd and 3rd division leagues and U18 — not top-tier broadcast setups. Cameras are consumer-grade, mounted badly, and move constantly during games.
  • Owned the entire pipeline: data collection, labeling, training, deployment, and the stats algorithms on top.

1) The Challenge — Bad Cameras, Moving Footage

  • Lower division football doesn't have top-quality production. Cameras shake, zoom in and out, cut between angles, and footage quality is far from broadcast standard.
  • Camera movement breaks naive tracking — a player's position changes even when they're standing still if the camera pans. All metrics that depend on position (speed, distance) have to account for this.
  • Models trained on clean, high-resolution data don't generalize. Had to build the training set from the actual footage — same cameras, same conditions.

2) Data Pipeline — Collect, Label, Train, Deploy

  • Collect: extracted frames from match recordings across multiple games and camera setups.
  • Label: annotated players, ball, and relevant zones — handling occlusion, motion blur, and the variety of lighting conditions in different stadiums.
  • Train: trained detection models on this domain-specific dataset so they perform reliably on the actual camera quality and conditions.
  • Deploy: built the inference pipeline to process match video and output structured data per player per frame.

3) Player Detection & Tracking

  • Detected and tracked every player across frames throughout the match — maintaining consistent identity even through occlusions, camera cuts, and movement.
  • Tracked the ball separately and linked ball position to nearby players for possession and pass detection.
  • Compensated for camera movement so that tracked positions map to real field coordinates, not raw pixel positions that shift with every pan or zoom.

4) Performance Stats — Speed, Distance, Possession, Passes

  • Speed: computed from positional delta per frame, calibrated against real field dimensions using the pitch lines as reference scale.
  • Distance travelled: accumulated over the full match per player, separating walking, jogging, and sprinting phases.
  • Ball possession: detected which player controls the ball at any given moment, aggregated over the match for individual and team totals.
  • Passes: detected pass events — ball leaving one player and arriving at another — with success/fail classification and direction.

5) Player Database & Stats Storage

  • All stats are stored per player per match — building a historical database that accumulates performance data over the season.
  • Enables comparison across games, tracking of player development, and data-driven selection decisions for the coaching staff.

What I Did

  • Built the full data pipeline from scratch: collection, labeling, training, and deployment.
  • Trained detection models on domain-specific data to handle low-quality, moving footage.
  • Built player and ball tracking with camera-motion compensation.
  • Developed algorithms for speed, distance, possession, and pass detection.
  • Designed and populated a player stats database for match-by-match performance tracking.
