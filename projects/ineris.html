Image Processing Internship at INERIS

February 2023 to August 2023 · Internship · Verneuil-en-Halatte, France

Python Computer Vision YOLOv5 Optical Flow STM32 Digital Filters Drones Embedded

Overview

  • 6-month internship at INERIS on the DESIHR research project: building a system to send a fleet of autonomous drones into large industrial fires and measure the pollution emitted in real time.
  • Worked on two parts: image processing and computer vision for the drones, and embedded signal processing for CO sensors on the drone hardware.
  • Achieved 92% precision in fire and smoke detection, 0.067 MSE on optical flow, and reduced CO sensor rise time from 71ms to 21ms with 1% overshoot.

1) The Mission

  • When a big factory fire breaks out, the goal is to send drones to map the pollution cloud and calculate the quantities of pollutants released.
  • Worked with thermodynamic and environmental engineers to understand their equations and find out exactly what data the drones needed to collect:
  • Shape of the fire.
  • Shape of the smoke plume.
  • Speed of the smoke at different distances from the fire.
  • Temperature of several points in the smoke.
  • CO sensor readings, cleaned of noise.

2) CO Sensors and STM32 Filtering

  • Programmed STM32 microcontrollers to acquire CO sensor data and apply digital filters in real time.
  • Designed filters that reduced the signal rise time from 71ms to 21ms, with only 1% overshoot and no noise on the output.
  • Built a small desktop software to test and visualize the sensor output in the lab before deploying on the drone.

Signal before and after the digital filter on the same graph.

Desktop software used to test the CO sensors in the lab.

3) Computer Vision - Fire and Smoke Detection

  • First tested a method from a research paper that used a neural network to segment fire pixels. With post-processing filters to remove noise and small regions, it gave decent results on close, clear footage.
  • But it failed at distance, in fog, and could not detect smoke. It was not reliable enough for real conditions.

Raw fire image used as input.

Segmentation output from the paper's neural network.

  • Switched to YOLOv5. Trained on public datasets plus our own footage. The model detected both fire and smoke with 92% precision, even in fog, shadow, and clouds.
  • This was a proof of concept, so no further training iterations were needed once the precision target was reached.

YOLOv5 detections: fire and smoke under various conditions.

4) Smoke Speed with Optical Flow

  • To measure smoke speed at different distances from the fire, tested several optical flow algorithms: DeepFlow, Farneback, SimpleFlow, and RAFT.
  • Evaluated them on real fire footage. RAFT gave the best results with a mean square error of 0.067.

Optical flow velocity vectors overlaid on smoke footage.

5) Temperature with Thermal Camera

  • Used a thermal camera mounted on the drone to measure temperature at multiple points in the smoke plume.
  • Temperature data feeds directly into the environmental engineers' pollution equations.

6) Full Pipeline

  • Combined all the outputs (fire/smoke shape from YOLO, smoke speed from RAFT, temperature from the thermal camera, CO readings from the STM32) into a single pipeline.
  • The pipeline inserts all values into the thermodynamic equations to produce a real-time pollution map of the fire site.

7) Field Tests in Normandy

  • Organized and participated in a 3-day field test in Le Havre, Normandy, to validate the full system with real fires under realistic conditions.
  • Ran the complete pipeline live and verified that all sensors, vision models, and data processing worked together on site.

At the test site with the drone fleet — Le Havre, 2023.

My Contribution

  • Worked with engineers to define what data the drones needed to collect.
  • Programmed STM32 boards and designed digital filters for CO sensor acquisition.
  • Built a lab tool to test and validate the sensors before field deployment.
  • Tested a segmentation-based approach for fire detection, identified its limits, and switched to YOLOv5.
  • Trained YOLOv5 on mixed data (public + our own) to detect fire and smoke at 92% precision.
  • Evaluated multiple optical flow algorithms on real fire footage and selected the best one.
  • Built the full pipeline combining all data sources into the pollution calculation.
  • Organized and participated in the 3-day field tests in Le Havre.
