Why I Love Intelligent Robots

How a childhood love for inventions became my path into electronics, computer vision, AI, and intelligent robots.

Since I was a kid, I was always interested in new inventions.

Even when I was watching cartoons, I was not only watching the story. I was watching the smart characters who were building machines, solving problems, and creating new tools. In Ninja Turtles, I liked watching Donatello invent things. In Mickey Mouse magazine, I liked Gyro Gearloose because he was always creating something new.

For me, invention was always exciting. The idea that a person can imagine something, build it, test it, improve it, and make it useful is one of the most interesting things in the world.

I never wanted to do only routine work. I did not want to repeat the same tasks every day without creating anything. I wanted to build, invent, think, test, fail, improve, and have an impact.

This is why intelligent robots became very special to me.

A robot is not only hardware. It is not only software. It is the meeting point between many worlds: electronics, mechanics, sensors, computer vision, artificial intelligence, control, and real world testing. A robot has to understand the world around it, make decisions, and act in a physical environment. That is what makes it exciting.

To follow this path, I studied electronics in my bachelor degree, then I continued with a master degree in Intelligent Robots and Information Processing. I wanted to understand both sides: the physical system and the intelligence inside it.

One of the moments that made my passion even stronger was during my bachelor, when I worked with my colleagues on a robot for the Coupe de France de Robotique. It was not only a school project. It was a real challenge, with real problems, real constraints, and real teamwork.

That experience made me understand something clearly: I did not become an engineer just to have a job. I became an engineer because I enjoy building things.

For me, intelligent robots are not only machines. They are a way to turn ideas into reality.
