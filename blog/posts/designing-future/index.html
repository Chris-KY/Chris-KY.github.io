Building the Future While Exploring the Present

A personal look at curiosity, learning, travel, sport, and the mindset that shapes how I work and live.

If I had to describe myself with one word, I would choose curiosity.

I like to understand how things work. I like to learn new topics, ask questions, test ideas, and explore. This is one of the reasons why I enjoy being an engineer. In engineering, there is always something new to learn. A new tool, a new method, a new problem, a new system, or a new way to think.

This is also why I like working in computer vision, AI, robotics, and intelligent systems. These fields are always moving. You cannot stay still. You have to keep learning, improving, and adapting. For me, that is not a problem. That is the fun part.

I also try to keep this same mindset in my personal life.

I love to travel and discover new places. Until now, I have visited almost 30 countries. For me, travel is not only about seeing beautiful places. It is also about discovering new cultures, new ways of thinking, and new ways of living. Every country teaches you something different.

I also like activities that challenge the mind and the body. I play chess and have a rating around 1300 on Chess.com. I play rugby, not professionally, but with real enjoyment. I also played field hockey before at a professional level.

All these things are different, but they have something in common. They all require learning, discipline, adaptation, and curiosity.

This is how I like to live and work. I do not want to only follow a fixed path. I want to explore, build, learn, and create. Sometimes the future is not fully clear, but that is also what makes it interesting.

For me, building the future starts by exploring the present.
