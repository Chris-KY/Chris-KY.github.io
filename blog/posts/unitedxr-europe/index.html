A Few Days at UnitedXR Europe

I went to Brussels for UnitedXR Europe. Three days of talks and discussions about where XR is going.

UnitedXR Europe is a three-day event in Brussels focused on XR, the metaverse, and everything that comes with it. I went there to listen, discover, and see what people in this space are actually thinking about right now.

One of the sessions that stayed with me was "Defining You: Digital Identity in the Metaverse" by Marine Boulot. The question she was exploring is simple but not easy to answer: who are you in a virtual space? In real life, identity is built through your face, your body, the way you move and react. When you are an avatar, all of that changes. How do you express emotions? How do you build trust with someone you have never really seen?

The second session I want to mention is "Choose-Your-Own-Reality: Ethics, Identity and XR + AI." This one was a panel with Alina Kadlubsky, Rob Morgan, Linda Ricci Jacobs, and Anastasia Sergeeva. They talked about a scenario that feels close: what happens when someone else can modify how you appear in XR? Who controls your image? One line from the discussion hit me: "if they apply a filter in XR, is it really different from the mental image they already have?" That question stuck with me after the event.

I work in computer vision and XR, so these topics are not abstract for me. But hearing them discussed from a social, ethical, and human angle was a good reminder that the technology is only one part of the equation. The harder questions are about people.
